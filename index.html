About me

Hello! I am Søren Sandager Sørensen, and most recently I was a postdoc at Aarhus University within the project Grammar in Everyday Life (PI: Jakob Steensig) until 2023. I work with grammar in interaction, and my studies include phenomena such as the functions of prosody and phonetics in interaction and response tokens/interjections in Danish, but I am also interested in grammar and phonology in general. I am also into linguistic typology and theory, and I am eager to work with languages other than Danish, and other approaches than I have so far!

I wrote my PhD thesis (finished 2020) on The Prosody of Response Tokens in Danish, and I have been part of the research groups DanTIN (Danish Talk-in-Interaction) and Sounds of Language and Speech.

At the moment, I am open to discussions about possible collaborations, projects and funding opportunities. If you know about a position you think I would fit in, let me know! Ideas for the future are welcome. Do not hesitate to contact me! Mail me at mails@sorensandagersorensen.dk.

I am currently working on topics related to:
  • Response tokens and other one-word-constructions and their functions
  • Swear words in Danish, other assessments and their syntax
  • Copula drop and drop of har 'has' in Danish - and what it says about both phonology, syntax and action

About me

Hi there! I am Søren Sandager Sørensen, and I am currently a postdoc at the University of Agder in Kristiansand, Norway (until 2027)! This position is part of the project Police investigative interviews in Norwegian as a Second Language (PI: Jan Svennevig), but I also work with grammar in interaction, language in context, and my interests also include prosody/phonetics, response tokens/interjections, and the relation between "small" and "big" units, in interaction and elsewhere. My interests also include linguistic typology and theory, and I am eager to work with languages other than Danish, and other approaches than I have so far!

Earlier, I have been part of the project Grammar in Everyday Life (PI: Jakob Steensig) until 2023. I wrote my PhD thesis (finished 2020) on The Prosody of Response Tokens in Danish. I am currently working on topics related to:

  • Response tokens and other one-word-constructions and their functions
  • Swear words in Danish, other assessments and their syntax
  • Copula variation
  • Repair and preemptive repair strategies
